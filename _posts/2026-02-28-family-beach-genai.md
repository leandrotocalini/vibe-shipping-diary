I've been deep in the agent world — text in, code out, PRs merged, daemons restarted. All text. All the time. But GenAI has a whole other side I hadn't touched yet: images.

So I did the simplest experiment I could think of. I took a photo of my family on the beach in Brazil and asked a model to turn us into anime characters.

## The Result

The output nailed things I didn't expect. My daughter's pigtails. The açaí stand behind us (complete with the "Aqui Tem Açaí" banner). The beach umbrellas, the sand, the green hills in the background. It captured the vibe of a Brazilian beach day — not just the people, but the place.

It also got things slightly wrong in the way GenAI images always do. Proportions that are almost right but not quite. A tattoo that moved. Details that feel like a dream version of the real thing — close enough to recognize, different enough to notice.

My daughter looked at it and laughed for five minutes. That alone was worth it.

## Why This Matters (To Me)

I'm not building an image product. I'm not pivoting to AI art. But playing with image generation reminded me of something I keep forgetting: the best way to understand a technology is to use it for something you care about.

I care about my family. I care about this trip. Turning a beach photo into an anime scene isn't a business case — it's a moment. And the fact that I can do it with a single prompt, in seconds, from my phone, still feels like magic.

Text generation stopped feeling like magic months ago. I use it every day — it's a tool now. Image generation still has that spark. I want to chase that feeling a bit more before it fades too.

## What's Next

I want to try more of this. Family photos, travel moments, maybe turning my daughter's drawings into "real" illustrations. Not because it's useful. Because it's fun, and because fun is how you actually learn what a technology can and can't do.

The agents can wait. Sometimes you just want to see your family in anime.
